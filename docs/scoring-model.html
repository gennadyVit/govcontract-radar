Opportunity Scoring Model

How fit scores are computed, gated, and converted to bid decisions.

Formula

FIT_SCORE = weighted sum of 5 components (0–100 each)
0.35× capability_similarity
0.25× past_performance_match
0.15× contract_size_fit
0.15× competition_score
0.10× strategic_fit

Components

Capability Similarity
35%
Cosine similarity between the opportunity's embedding and the company profile embedding. Measures whether the company can actually do the work — the most important signal.
Past Performance Match
25%
Does the company have prior work in this NAICS code and agency? Federal RFPs formally score past performance — a company with no relevant history is heavily penalized in evaluation.
Contract Size Fit
15%
Is the estimated contract value within the company's comfortable range? Too small = margin risk. Too large = execution risk. Scores 100 when within range, degrades proportionally outside it.
Competition Score
15%
Small business historical win rate for this NAICS code, plus a bonus if the set-aside type matches company certifications. Harder to win NAICS codes score lower.
Strategic Fit
10%
Keyword overlap between the opportunity text and the company's stated focus areas. A weak signal on its own — used as a tiebreaker, not a primary factor.

Hard Gates

Applied after the weighted score. Gates cap the score — they surface the opportunity with a warning rather than hiding it.

CAP → 40 Opportunity requires a set-aside (8(a), WOSB, HUBZone, etc.) that the company does not hold
CAP → 50 Required security clearance exceeds company's current clearance level
CAP → 60 Onsite-only requirement in a location the company cannot support
CAP → 65 Contract value is more than 10× the company's maximum comfortable contract size

Future Refinement

Weights are defaults, not validated. Once real bid history exists (won/lost outcomes), the 5 component weights can be tuned using logistic regression against actual results. The current values reflect reasonable priors about federal contracting — capability and past performance dominate, keyword matching is a weak signal — but the right weights for a specific company may differ significantly.
